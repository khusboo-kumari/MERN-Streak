Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and missile development programs, notably the development of ballistic missiles and launch vehicle technology at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He was instrumental in the success of India's nuclear tests in 1998. Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam was an inspiring leader, dedicated to education and youth empowerment, earning him the title of the "People's President." His humility, wisdom, and vision for a self-reliant India left an indelible mark on the nation's development. He passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire millions.
